NPRR1301: Modifications to ECRS Procurement Methodology

REVISION REQUEST NUMBER: NPRR1301
SUBMITTING ENTITY: NRG Energy, Inc.
DATE SUBMITTED: April 28, 2026
STATUS: Filed — Pending PRS Review

I. REVISION SUMMARY

This Nodal Protocol Revision Request (NPRR1301) proposes amendments to
Sections 6.5.7 and 6.5.9 of the ERCOT Nodal Operating Protocols to revise the
methodology used by ERCOT to procure ERCOT Contingency Reserve Service (ECRS)
during periods of low system inertia.

II. BACKGROUND

ECRS was introduced as a new ancillary service product in June 2023 to provide
10-minute headroom for ERCOT to manage contingency events without depleting
Responsive Reserve Service (RRS). Since ECRS's introduction, operational
experience across multiple seasons has revealed that the current procurement
formula systematically underestimates ECRS requirements during high-wind,
low-inertia conditions that commonly occur during overnight hours when solar
generation is unavailable and wind generation is concentrated in West Texas.

During the winter 2025-2026 season, ERCOT issued three Emergency Notification
System (ENS) activations that post-event analysis attributed in part to
insufficient ECRS procurement during low-inertia periods. These events motivated
NRG to propose structural modifications to the procurement formula.

III. PROPOSED PROTOCOL CHANGES

This revision proposes three changes to the ERCOT Nodal Operating Protocols:

1. Inertia Adjustment Factor (Section 6.5.7(c) — new subsection):
   Add a new inertia adjustment factor (IAF) to the ECRS procurement formula.
   The IAF shall be calculated as a function of real-time system inertia measured
   in MW-seconds (MWs). When system inertia falls below 150,000 MWs, the IAF
   increases the base ECRS procurement requirement by a graduated percentage
   not to exceed 25 percent.

2. Minimum ECRS Procurement Floor (Section 6.5.7(d) — new subsection):
   Establish minimum ECRS procurement floors of 2,500 MW during identified
   high-risk periods (defined as hours with system inertia below 100,000 MWs
   and wind penetration exceeding 60 percent of real-time load).

3. DAM Schedule Publication (Section 6.5.9):
   Require ERCOT to publish a revised ECRS procurement schedule in the
   Day-Ahead Market timeline, no later than 10:00 AM CT of the day prior to the
   operating day, to allow market participants to plan resource commitments.

IV. MARKET IMPACT AND COST ESTIMATE

Increased ECRS procurement will reduce capacity available for energy offers from
resources providing ECRS. The submitting entity estimates:
- Average annual ancillary service cost increase: $85-120 million based on
  historical ECRS clearing prices from June 2023 through March 2026.
- Reliability benefit: estimated reduction of 2-3 ENS activations per year based
  on simulation analysis of the winter 2025-2026 events.

NRG contends that improved system reliability offsets the cost increase by
reducing the frequency of high-priced real-time scarcity events.

V. PROPOSED EFFECTIVE DATE

If approved by the PUCT Board: October 1, 2026, to align with the start of the
winter 2026-2027 season.
